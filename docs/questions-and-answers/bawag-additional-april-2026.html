How long has the property been used as a refugee centre? Is this accommodation or administrative?

Since 2022. It is accommodation (not administrative).

What do we expect NOI to be if the municipal space was re-let in a traditional sense?

The municipality pays EUR 1m/yr at EUR 315/m². Comparable commercial/industrial tenants in the same complex pay EUR 50–175/m². Re-letting at ~EUR 100/m² would yield ~EUR 317k, reducing total NOI from EUR 2,290k to ~EUR 1,607k. No cash sweep or amortisation. Covenants are LTV 65% and ICR 1.5x (current actual ICR: 3.80x). Not considered necessary given the low risk profile.

Additional Loans

RAX 504; Median / HEMA Den Bosch

Is the building only listed because of the contents of the basement? If the ornaments were relocated would the listed status be removed?

The property is in a protected cityscape (beschermd stadsgezicht) and is a municipal monument. Use is tied to the existing structure and appearance. Given the location, retail is an appropriate and fitting designation; it is an excellent retail location and there is no need for de-listing. The listing does not prevent facade renovation.

Has HEMA occupied the entire building since 1930 or have they taken and given back various other parts through that time? Why are they now downsizing?

The current lease dates from 2005; HEMA likely occupied the building before that but we cannot confirm with certainty. HEMA is not downsizing; they extended the lease to 2035 with four further 10-year renewal options and are investing in a "HEMA 100" format renovation (similar to their Maastricht and Leeuwarden flagship stores). They still lease the entire building.

What's the Borrower's source of cash for the incentives in the allonge?

The incentives total ~EUR 1.1m (EUR 820k investment contribution, ~EUR 180k rent-free period, and up to EUR 100k facade share). Funded from cash flow of the borrower's operating businesses and the sale of a residential property.

How do Dutch second mortgages work? Can the 1st charge remove that 2nd charge in case of enforcement and sale or can the 2nd charge block any sale by refusing to release?

Under Dutch mortgage law, a first-ranking mortgagee can enforce and sell free and clear of junior liens. The second mortgagee cannot block the sale; they only have a claim on residual proceeds. There is a second mortgage on this property in favour of Koppelaar I B.V.

How is the junior loan subordinated? Contractual or structural?

Both contractual and structural.

Is RAX taking any security over the subordinated instruments?

Yes. Subordination per RAX standard contract terms: no right of immediate enforcement, full subordination, prohibition on demand for repayment, and an obligation to cooperate with discharge of the mortgage.

RAX 509; Varod Street Rotterdam

Who is the long leaseholder? What are the terms of the leasehold? Is this a peppercorn interest or geared based on income? Any forfeiture rights? Any mortgagee protection? At what point do leaseholds begin to lose value?

The ground lease (erfpacht) is from the municipality of Rotterdam. It is a temporary erfpacht, pre-paid until 2097. With ~71 years remaining, the leasehold term has no impact on value. Dutch erfpacht does not suffer the same cliff-edge depreciation that affects English leasehold interests; there is no equivalent "80-year" threshold effect.

Are there any automatic or legally mandated rights of extension on leasehold interests?

Addressed above; pre-paid until 2097, no near-term renewal required.

Any update on the hotel redevelopment angle?

Zoning ("Gemengd – 3") permits hotel/hospitality use. The borrower is progressing the permit application.

How much is the private guarantee for? Is that interest only or principal as well? Any restrictions on calling the guarantee?

Abstract guarantee, capped at EUR 200,000.

Amsterdam Noord is leasehold until 2047? As above what happens to the security when that matures?

The Amsterdam property is held on a perpetual ground lease (voortdurend erfpacht), pre-paid until 2047. At that point the canon will be re-assessed. The property is likely to be redeveloped before then, at which point a new canon assessment or buyout will take place. The loan matures September 2026, well before any erfpacht reassessment.

How does the Power of Sale work? What is this agreement? Are there any restrictions or triggers that need to occur to utilise the PoS?

The lender has the contractual right to sell the property privately (onderhands) in case of default, avoiding the public auction process. This typically achieves a better sale price than forced auction.
